What?

Omahack is a 24-hour hackathon in Omaha, NE, on April 7-8, 2023. We believe talented people often miss the opportunity to bump shoulders with others who compliment their skillsets and enable powerful teams and relationships. More details on the event location and schedule will be coming soon!

More Questions and Answers...

What's the cost?

Admission and team participation is free and includes mentors, workshops, resources, and a great time!

What's the schedule?

Omahack will take place on April 7-8, 2023 (Friday to Saturday). Join the mailing list above for future updates.

But I've never hacked before!

That's okay! Hackathon teams need people with many skills — writers, artists, designers, and many others are always welcome to pitch their skills to a team.

Have more questions?

Contact us at omahackhq@gmail.com with any other questions or concerns.

Teams

How do teams work?

Teams will self-organize ahead of team registration using the Omahack Slack channel and the Omahack Github teams repository. This process will begin soon!

What if I don't have a team?

Don't worry! In addition to the team formation process described above, there will be a team formation opportunity immediately before the event begins where people can pitch ideas and team up.

Mentors & Judges

Can I volunteer to be a mentor?

Of course! In addition to ambitious teams, we need mentors who can offer their skills and experience to teams and float around the event helping out as they can. Mentors can offer advice, product validation, and play the role of a potential first customer during the discovery and development process. Mentors should plan to be mingling and available while at the event and will be a key part of keeping the energy positive. Please contact us at omahackhq@gmail.com if you're interested in learning more about volunteering as a mentor.

Can I volunteer to be a judge?

The judging panel will composed of Omaha community members, business leaders, and others in the Omaha startup ecosystem who can ask the "hard questions" following each pitch and help to select the most interesting and viable team projects at the end of the event. Please contact us at omahackhq@gmail.com if you're interested in learning more about volunteering as a judge.

On Failure...

Do I have to submit a project at the end of the hackathon?

Yes! We understand not all project efforts end in success and that is the second best part about hackathons -- we fail fast and pivot! We strongly encourage everyone to pitch their team's results, lessons learned, and ideas for future attempts.
